Content Management Systems (CMS)

After 3 years (beginning 2008, beginning 2011) company needs have changed from simple ASP.net 2.0 application with company data and few texts/cheat-sheets to need for fully fledged CMS system.

Most popular, open and free and widely spread CMS systems are WordPress, Drupal and Joomla(as of 2011-05)
http://trends.builtwith.com/cms
But most of those systems are PHP based and due to the fact that it is hard to have profound knowledge and skills in several technologies (due to the finite nature of time person has at disposal), team made decision to stick with familiar technology - ASP.net.
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_content_management_systems http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_web_application_frameworks
Next requirements dictated smaller, modular system preferably without database to reduce maintenance efforts and thus the costs, so the search revealed Composite C1...
